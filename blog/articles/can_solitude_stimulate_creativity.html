Can solitude stimulate creativity? 3/10/2017

In a time like ours, where social networks are essential, the willingness to be alone can be considered in our societies as strange a behavior! Workers of the tertiary sector are paradoxically closed in “open-spaces”, and frequently meetings are called to “analyze something” (literally).

Groups dynamic can me stimulating; it can help to unite teams. But can that way of organizing work be s good way to create a creative dynamic? It seems to be the opposite. According to an extract of a research published by the New York Times, all those procedures could damage creativity. In fact, during group meetings, introverted creative people might keep their ideas for them, afraid of expressing them. That creates a group dynamic with leaders that have followers as well as passive people, this oversteps the creativity dynamic, more reduced.

The fact of having the choice of being isolated has a crucial importance for the development of creativity. The requests of others and distractions are constant, and concentration is very fragile. It is important for the creator to have a private place and to be convinced that he is acting freely.

That isolation is therefore necessary for almost every step of the creation, but it entails a particular importance during the most dynamic moments of the creational action (the appearance of ideas and its execution).

In his letters with Faydeau, Flaubert remarked that solitude was necessary for him in order to get to work, having on him the effect of an excitant: “you would like to know that I have been completely alone for eight days now. I work without interruption, until 4 a.m. every night. I can see how that is starting to work; I mean that I am starting to have fun. I sing alone and outload around the different rooms of my house, until I can no longer stand it”.

Solitude is necessary if you want to have a prolific creative activity. However, be isolated when one finds it necessary is a luxury. If it is not something you desire, but something that is imposed to you it can drive you crazy…Humans are social animals, they have the need to interact with other counterparts who are crucial for creativity as well as for the well-being. Solitude is something that has to be chosen, and the artist must organize his activities in order to be able to enjoy it when he has that need.

We make reference here to the execution of the creative idea, the way on which it materializes. Social interactions, particularly belonging to a community where creativity is appreciated makes part of the stimulant factors of the creative impetus
